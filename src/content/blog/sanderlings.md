Sanderlings are small, plump shorebirds found along sandy beaches and coastal areas. You might know them as the little birds that skitter back and forth with the waves.

I first encountered them visiting the beaches off La Jolla, California. I had never seen such tiny, energetic birds before. They are about the size of a sparrow, with pale gray and white plumage that helps them blend into their sandy surroundings.

![A group of sanderlings](../../assets/images/sanderlings_together.jpg)

They're easy to mistake for other small shorebirds. In fact, when I first started photographing them, an elderly couple nearby told me they were "Snowy Plovers." After a quick check in my bird guide, I confirmed they were indeed sanderlings.
